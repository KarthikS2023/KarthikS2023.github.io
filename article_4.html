    As of 2022, the estimate for the global esports market reached $1.45 billion, with projections of further growth to $1.72 billion by the end of 2023 (1). This growth is fueled by prominent gaming companies such as Nintendo and Tencent, which have experienced great revenue surges in the last few years and are expected to remain dominant over the market (1). Smaller gambling companies are entering strategic collaborations with sports organizations, hosting virtual tournaments, while others are stepping up with charity tournaments to help deal with problems such as the spread of viruses (1). WIthin the professional gaming industry, earnings vary colossally, with most players earning $1000-$5000 per month, and $12,000-$60,000 per year (2).
